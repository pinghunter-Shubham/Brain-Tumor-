  2. Tumor Classification

    3. We focus on three primary types of brain tumors:

    • Glioma Tumor

      • Gliomas are fast-growing tumors that originate from glial cells in the brain or spinal cord. These cells provide crucial support and protection for neurons. Gliomas are the most common and aggressive type of brain tumor, often leading to severe neurological impairments. Early diagnosis is critical to managing the tumor and improving patient outcomes, typically through surgery, radiation, or chemotherapy.

    • Meningioma Tumor

      • Meningiomas develop from the meninges, the protective membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord. These tumors are generally slow-growing and often benign, though their size and location can cause significant complications, such as headaches and vision problems. Early detection helps to prevent long-term damage, and treatment can include surgical removal if necessary.

    • Pituitary Tumor

      • Pituitary tumors occur in the pituitary gland, affecting the regulation of essential hormones. Although most pituitary tumors are benign, they can lead to serious health issues due to hormonal imbalances. Early diagnosis allows for appropriate treatment, including medications, surgery, or radiation, to manage the tumor and its effects on the body.
