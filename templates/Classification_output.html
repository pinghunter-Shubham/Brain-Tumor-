Glioma Tumor: Gliomas are tumors that arise from glial cells in the brain or spine. They are classified based on their location and the type of glial cells involved. Gliomas can vary in aggressiveness, with some being relatively slow-growing and others highly malignant. Common types include astrocytomas, oligodendrogliomas, and ependymomas.

Meningioma Tumor: Meningiomas are tumors that develop from the meninges, the protective membranes covering the brain and spinal cord. They are often slow-growing and typically benign, though they can cause symptoms by pressing on nearby brain structures. Treatment usually involves surgical removal, and in some cases, radiation therapy.

Pituitary Tumor: Pituitary tumors originate in the pituitary gland, which is located at the base of the brain and regulates various hormonal functions. These tumors can be non-cancerous (benign) or, rarely, cancerous (malignant). Symptoms often result from hormonal imbalances or pressure on surrounding structures, and treatment may involve surgery, medication, or radiation therapy.
